Interactively Exploring Publications for Open Celled Metal Foam

Jessica Hills and Rebecca Mih

The chord diagram is useful to visualize the contents of documents. In this case, academic publications for metal foams are classified by the materials used to make the foam (Aluminum, Copper, etc), and materials properties (such as strength, elasticity, conductivity etc.) that were tested by the authors. In this way, it's easy to what materials and properties researchers have studied the most, by the relative size of the arcs and chords in the diagram. The chord diagram was created using D3.js library.

Analysis of the most prolific authors, and the most popular journals, by material and properties, is helpful for identifying potential collaborators and their areas of expertise. These plots were created using Chart.js library.

This interactive bubble chart shows the evolution of research interest in metal foams, over time. Citations are the number of times an article has be referenced in a later publication. The number of citations by others indicates the importance of the articles content. Seminal, ground-breaking articles may have hundreds or even thousands of citations. The plot was created using the plotly library.
